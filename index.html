/ What Made Me Who I Am Today

From a very young age, I dreamed of becoming a professional ice hockey player. For me, hockey was more than just a sport, it was my sanctuary, a place where I could dream big and work hard to chase those dreams.

In the beginning, it was all about having fun, making friends, and staying active. But as I got older, the competition grew tougher, and earning a spot on the team became more challenging. Between the ages of 16 and 20, I played at the highest level for juniors. I attended schools that included hockey training as part of the curriculum, practicing 4–5 days a week and playing games 1–2 days each weekend. I dedicated countless hours to becoming the best player I could be.

Eventually, my priorities shifted. Hockey wasn’t as fun anymore, and I became interested in exploring other areas of life. When I decided to leave the sport, I had no regrets. The years I spent sweating, sacrificing, and striving were invaluable.

Hockey taught me lessons I carry with me every day. I learned how to set personal goals, work as part of a team, handle wins and losses, and stay dedicated and competitive. Most importantly, I discovered how to play hard, stay fair, and enjoy the journey along the way.

/ From Boy To Man

In 2020, I began my military service at F17 as a Flygbassäkerhetssoldat. Over the course of nine transformative months, I served as a group leader, managing and guiding a team of eight. This experience shaped me in many ways, both personally and professionally.

During my time in the military, I developed important skills like leadership, teamwork, problem-solving, and time management. I learned how to push limits, both my own and others, and gained a strong understanding of what it takes to lead under pressure. Most importantly, Most importantly, my confidence grew, ready to handle challenges with focus and determination.

After completing my initial service, I accepted a part-time role as a soldier. I now take part in 2–3 military exercises each year, ranging from small-scale drills to larger operations. These activities give me the chance to improve my skills further, with training in leadership, weapon handling, and strategy. Being part of a growing military force that works to protect peace and Sweden’s borders is both exciting and rewarding.
